Parliamentary Briefing on Digital Sovereignty (Tweede Kamer)

Based on a series of academic papers and policy analyses on digital sovereignty, I was invited to inform members of the Dutch House of Representatives (Tweede Kamer) on the implications of extraterritorial legislation, cloud dependency, and state-level digital autonomy.

This contribution translated academic research into actionable policy insights, bridging software engineering, cybersecurity governance, and public administration. The briefing focused on the structural risks that arise when public-sector digital infrastructure and communications depend on foreign-controlled platforms and legal jurisdictions.

  • Role: Author and briefing contributor
  • Context: Parliamentary policy discussion (Netherlands)
  • Focus: Digital sovereignty, cloud governance, extraterritorial law
  • Academic relevance: Demonstrates impact beyond the classroom through policy engagement
